About Us

Wayne Enterprises

Wayne Enterprises isn’t just a company it's a Legacy Written in Steel and Stone

A City Worth Fighting For

Wayne Enterprises was founded in 1870 by Alan Wayne, one of Gotham’s earliest visionaries. During Gotham’s industrial boom, it grew into a cornerstone of the city, shaping its identity with towering skyscrapers, intricate railways, and groundbreaking infrastructure.

Under Dr. Thomas Wayne’s leadership in the mid-20th century, Wayne Enterprises shifted its focus. By the 1960s, the company wasn’t just about profits—it was about people. Projects like Gotham General Hospital. But the city’s darkness struck in 1981, when Thomas and Martha Wayne were killed in Crime Alley after a fateful encounter with Joe Chill. Some say the Joker’s chaos set the stage for the city’s unraveling, casting shadows over their untimely deaths.

Inheriting his parents' vision and burden, Bruce Wayne returned to Gotham in the late 1990s. He turned Wayne Enterprises into a symbol of hope, launching initiatives in clean energy, urban renewal, and cutting-edge technology.

Wayne Enterprises stood tall during Gotham’s darkest days, from the chaos unleashed by Ra’s al Ghul and Bane’s siege of the city to the aftermath of the Riddler’s catastrophic flood in 2022. Whether rebuilding bridges or lighting the city’s path forward, Wayne Enterprises is more than just a corporation. It’s the lifeline of Gotham—a reminder that even in the shadow of despair, there’s always a way forward.
